The Flow keyboard layout is very different from the QWERTY layout you are probably familiar with. It is a very logical layout, though, and understanding the logic behind it is the key to learning it quickly. It is designed with one guiding principle in mind: allow common English words to be traced with the shortest, smoothest path possible.

All the vowels are grouped together in the middle of the keyboard. That's what you would expect, since almost every word contains vowels, and they are among the most common letters in the English language. They are also drawn in a different color to make them easier to see:

Try typing all of the vowels into this box:

Enter:
aeiou

Another important group of letters are found in the second row. They spell the word "therm":

A therm is a unit of heat, and these are very "hot" letters. For example, "the" is the most common 3-letter sequence in English, and "her" is the fourth most common.

Try typing the following words, which use only these letters. To type a word, touch the first letter. Without lifting your finger, move it to the second letter, then to the third letter, and so on. Once you reach the last letter in the word, lift your finger. Flow will add spaces between words automatically.

Enter:
the he her them me hem

Taken together, the letters in this row plus the vowels account for over 60% of all letters in English. Try typing the following words:

Enter:
that more heart reheat hour mite route item

Another very important letter sequence is "ing". In fact, this is the third most common 3-letter sequence in English. No surprise, then, that Flow places these letters right together, all arranged in a column:

Try typing the following words:

Enter:
thing ring ingot hearing training arranging grain range

Don't worry about the double r in "arranging". Just move your finger from a to r, and then on to the next letter. Flow will figure out that it needs to be a double letter.

"Th" is the most common 2-letter sequence in English, and as you've seen, Flow puts these letters right next to each other. "Sh", "ch", and "wh" are also common sequences, so it's no surprise that s, c, and w are all close to h:

Try typing some words that use these letters:

Enter:
what chess she short who where much wishing which

Another common letter sequence is "ld":

Try typing some words that use this sequence:

Enter:
would could sold holding cold

Taken together, the letters you have learned so far account for almost 90% of all letters in English. Try typing these words:

Enter:
sun old real nice air ice house shore mound

Here are some longer words. If you don't remember where the next letter is, just pause a moment and think which group it's part of.

Enter:
welcome candle treasure singer creation tradition accident

This leaves the less commonly used letters. They are arranged around the outside edge of the keyboard:

As much as possible, their positions still reflect how they are used. For example, q is close to u, which almost always follows it. K is next to c, since it often appears as part of the sequence "ck". Y is above the letters o and u, so the common letter sequence "you" forms a straight line down a column.

Try typing these words:

Enter:
by very plank exact quick fun just zoo young cake

Here are some longer words:

Enter:
please fumble quavery express juxtapose bookshop

There is one more "letter" we haven't talked about: apostrophe. It isn't really a letter, of course, but it can still appear in words, and Flow knows many common contractions.

To enter a contraction, just trace through the apostrophe key like any other letter. Try typing these words:

Enter:
he's I'm I'll it's can't won't wouldn't we've that's they're

Flow does not recognize possessives: words like "Bob's", "Sally's", "dog's", "cat's", etc. To enter a possessive, enter the word without the 's, lift your finger, then press ' and s as two separate keys.

You're all ready to start using Flow! To really become comfortable with the layout, you just need to practice. You may also want to review this tutorial from time to time. Enjoy typing!
